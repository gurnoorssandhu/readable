This session covered the fundamental concept of electric charge as presented in Purcell's Electricity and Magnetism textbook. The reader asked for a basic explanation of what electric charge is, and we explored its definition as a fundamental property of matter.

Key insights from the discussion included understanding charge as one of the basic "qualities" that building blocks of matter possess, existing in two varieties (positive and negative), and exhibiting fundamental properties of conservation and quantization.

The session also touched on the historical perspective of how electricity was initially seen as an "extraordinary phenomenon" or "subtle fire" before being recognized as fundamental to the physical and chemical properties of matter from atomic to cellular scales. The discussion established that electrical forces, governed by Coulomb's law, are responsible for most material properties we observe.
