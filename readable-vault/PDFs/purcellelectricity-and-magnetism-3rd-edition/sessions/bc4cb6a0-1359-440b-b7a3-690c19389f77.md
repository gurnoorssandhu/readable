This session focused on pages 28–33 of Purcell's *Electricity and Magnetism* (3rd Edition), covering two foundational topics in electrostatics: the quantization of electric charge and Coulomb's Law.

The reader first asked about charge quantization. The discussion established that all observable electric charge exists in discrete integer multiples of the elementary charge *e* ≈ 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C. Millikan's oil-drop experiment was highlighted as the key empirical confirmation, and the Dirac monopole argument was mentioned as a theoretical basis.

The reader then asked for an explanation of a passage on quarks (page 29). The session clarified that while quarks carry fractional charges (+2e/3 and −e/3), they are permanently confined within hadrons by quantum chromodynamics (QCD). The proton (uud) and neutron (udd) quark compositions were worked through explicitly, confirming that observable particles always carry integer multiples of *e*.

Finally, Figure 1.2 from page 31 was explained in detail. It illustrates Coulomb's Law in SI units with two positive charges (5 C and 2 C) separated by 10 m, yielding a repulsive force of ~8.988 × 10⁸ N. The figure underscores the textbook's point that one coulomb is an astronomically large practical unit of charge.
