This session focused on pages 29–39 of Purcell's *Electricity and Magnetism* (3rd Edition), covering the electrostatic potential energy of charge distributions, with particular attention to the NaCl crystal lattice calculation.

The reader asked about the origin of the 1/2 factor in the double-sum formula for total electrostatic potential energy. The assistant explained that the double sum over all pairs $j, k$ (with $j \neq k$) counts every pair twice — once as $(j,k)$ and once as $(k,j)$ — so the 1/2 corrects for this double-counting, ensuring each unique interaction pair contributes only once to the total energy $U$.

The reader then confirmed their understanding that $U$ in the NaCl lattice calculation represents the total electrical potential energy of the crystal. The assistant confirmed this, walking through how Purcell builds up the lattice energy by selecting a central ion, summing its Coulomb interactions with shells of neighboring ions at increasing distances (the 6 nearest Cl⁻ at distance $a$, 12 Na⁺ at $\sqrt{2}a$, 8 Cl⁻ at $\sqrt{3}a$, etc.), and then multiplying by $N/2$ to get the full crystal energy. The negative sign of the final result reflects that the crystal is electrostatically bound.
