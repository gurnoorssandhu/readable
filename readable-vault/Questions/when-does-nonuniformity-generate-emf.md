The session showed that spatial non-uniformity of B across the loop (B₁ ≠ B₂) is necessary for a net motional EMF when the loop moves at constant velocity through a static field. But the full Faraday law ε = −dΦ/dt suggests EMF can also arise from a changing loop area or a time-varying B. The question is: how do these three sources (field gradient, area change, time-varying B) combine in general, and is there a clean geometric way to see when they cancel or reinforce? This connects to the open question about [[when-is-an-induced-electric-field-motional-vs-faraday]].
