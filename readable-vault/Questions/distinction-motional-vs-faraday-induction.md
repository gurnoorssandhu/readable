In the moving rod discussed in [[purcellelectricity-and-magnetism-3rd-edition]] (Ch. 7), the internal electric field is purely electrostatic — arising from surface charge redistribution, not from a changing magnetic flux. Yet in other situations (e.g., a stationary loop in a changing B field), the induced E field is a genuine Faraday field with non-zero curl.

The distinction matters conceptually: motional EMF can be explained by magnetic forces on charges, while Faraday induction fields exist even without charge motion. A fuller treatment of this distinction would clarify when each picture applies and how they unify under the full Faraday's law.
