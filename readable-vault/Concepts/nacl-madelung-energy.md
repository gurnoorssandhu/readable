Purcell's calculation of the NaCl lattice energy (Eqs. 1.17–1.18 in [[purcellelectricity-and-magnetism-3rd-edition]]) illustrates how the [[electrostatic-potential-energy]] formula applies to a periodic ionic solid.

Starting from a central positive Na⁺ ion, successive shells of neighbors contribute:

| Shell | Ions | Distance | Contribution |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1st | 6 Cl⁻ | $a$ | $-6e^2/a$ (attractive) |
| 2nd | 12 Na⁺ | $\sqrt{2}\,a$ | $+12e^2/\sqrt{2}\,a$ (repulsive) |
| 3rd | 8 Cl⁻ | $\sqrt{3}\,a$ | $-8e^2/\sqrt{3}\,a$ (attractive) |

The alternating signs reflect the alternating arrangement of positive and negative ions in the FCC-based NaCl structure. Summing over all shells yields a convergent series (the Madelung series) with a negative total, meaning the lattice is bound. Multiplying by $N/2$ gives the total energy for $N$ ion pairs.
