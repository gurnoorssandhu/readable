When a conducting loop moves through a region where the magnetic field is spatially non-uniform, a net EMF is induced even though the loop's shape and speed are constant.

**Setup:** A rectangular loop moves with velocity **v** in the y-direction. The left side of the loop sits in field B₁ and the right side in field B₂, each side having length w.

**Which sides contribute?** Only the two sides perpendicular to the direction of motion contribute to the EMF. The top and bottom sides (parallel to motion) produce a magnetic force **f = qv × B** that is perpendicular to the wire segment **ds**, so **f · ds = 0** there.

**Net EMF (Eq. 7.4–7.5 in [[purcellelectricity-and-magnetism-3rd-edition]]):**
$$\mathcal{E} = v(B_1 - B_2)w$$

If the field were uniform (B₁ = B₂), the contributions from opposite sides would cancel exactly and no EMF would result. The non-uniformity is essential.

See also [[motional-emf-and-charge-separation-in-a-moving-rod]] for the single-rod case.
