One of the fundamental properties of electric charge identified in [[purcellelectricity-and-magnetism-3rd-edition]]. Charge conservation means that the total electric charge in an isolated system remains constant over time.

This principle is fundamental to understanding electrical phenomena and is one of the key properties that makes electric charge a conserved quantity in physics, alongside energy and momentum conservation.
