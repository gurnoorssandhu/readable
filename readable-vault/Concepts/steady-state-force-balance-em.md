In electromagnetic systems where charges are driven by a non-electric force (such as a magnetic force on a moving conductor), a steady state is reached when the resulting electric field exactly cancels the driving force on each charge.

For a rod moving at velocity **v** in field **B** (from [[purcellelectricity-and-magnetism-3rd-edition]], Ch. 7):

- Driving force: $\mathbf{f} = q\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{B}$
- Balancing electric force: $q\mathbf{E}$
- Equilibrium: $\mathbf{E} = -\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{B}$

This principle generalizes: in any conductor at equilibrium, the net force on charge carriers is zero. The analogy to a fully charged battery (where internal chemistry is balanced by the built-up voltage) is instructive.
