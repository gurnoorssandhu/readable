**Coulomb's Law** describes the electrostatic force between two point charges:

$$F = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \cdot \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2}$$

where $\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \approx 8.988 \times 10^9 \ \text{N m}^2/\text{C}^2$ and $\epsilon_0 \approx 8.854 \times 10^{-12} \ \text{C}^2/(\text{N m}^2)$.

- Like charges repel; unlike charges attract.
- The force vectors obey Newton's third law (equal magnitude, opposite direction).

**Example from Figure 1.2** ([[purcellelectricity-and-magnetism-3rd-edition]], p. 31): Two positive charges of 5 C and 2 C separated by 10 m produce a repulsive force of ~8.988 × 10⁸ N. This enormous value illustrates why the coulomb is an impractically large unit in everyday contexts.
