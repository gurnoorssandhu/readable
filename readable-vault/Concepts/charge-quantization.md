The property that electric charge comes in discrete, indivisible amounts rather than continuous values. As mentioned in [[purcellelectricity-and-magnetism-3rd-edition]], this is one of the fundamental characteristics that distinguishes electric charge.

Quantization means there is a smallest possible unit of charge (the elementary charge), and all observable charges are integer multiples of this fundamental unit.

### From session bc4cb6a0-1359-440b-b7a3-690c19389f77 (2026-04-15)

**Charge quantization** is the principle that electric charge exists only in discrete integer multiples of the elementary charge *e*:

$$q = n \cdot e, \quad n \in \mathbb{Z}, \quad e \approx 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \ \text{C}$$

This was experimentally confirmed by Millikan's oil-drop experiment (1909). Theoretically, Dirac's magnetic monopole argument provides one derivation from first principles.

**Quarks and fractional charges:** Quarks carry charges of +2e/3 or −e/3, but are permanently confined inside hadrons by QCD (see [[quark-confinement]]). Observable free particles always carry integer multiples of *e*, so quantization holds at the macroscopic level. Discussed in [[purcellelectricity-and-magnetism-3rd-edition]], pages 28–29.
