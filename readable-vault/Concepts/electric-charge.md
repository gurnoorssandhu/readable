A fundamental property or quality that the building blocks of matter possess. According to [[purcellelectricity-and-magnetism-3rd-edition]], electric charge is so fundamental that "the existence of this book is attributable" to this property.

## Key Characteristics:
- Exists in **two varieties**: positive and negative
- Exhibits **conservation** - charge is conserved in physical processes
- Exhibits **quantization** - charge comes in discrete amounts
- Particles with charge interact via the electric force (Coulomb's law)

## Fundamental Role:
Electrical forces "largely determine the physical and chemical properties of matter over the whole range from atom to living cell." What early investigators called "subtle fire" or saw as "extraordinary phenomena" is now understood to be a fundamental aspect of matter's behavior.

Historically, the study of stationary electric charges is called electrostatics.
