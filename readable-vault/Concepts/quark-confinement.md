Quarks are the sub-hadronic constituents of hadrons (e.g., protons and neutrons). They carry fractional electric charges:

| Quark type | Charge |
|---|---|
| Up-type (u) | +2e/3 |
| Down-type (d) | −e/3 |

**Proton (uud):** $\frac{2e}{3} + \frac{2e}{3} - \frac{e}{3} = +e$

**Neutron (udd):** $\frac{2e}{3} - \frac{e}{3} - \frac{e}{3} = 0$

Quantum chromodynamics (QCD) predicts that quarks cannot exist as free, isolated particles — they are permanently **confined** inside hadrons. No fractionally charged free particle has ever been conclusively observed. As a result, [[charge-quantization]] in integer multiples of *e* is preserved at all observable scales.
